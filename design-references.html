SmartSpend

Design Reference Board

These are different visual directions worth borrowing from. Pick the one that feels closest, or mix two together and I can redesign SmartSpend around it.

Suggested approach: choose one app for layout, one app for typography, and one app for color mood.
Direction 1

Apple

Best if you want it calm, premium, spacious, and minimal.

  • Large headings and generous whitespace
  • Very light backgrounds and soft borders
  • Simple cards with almost no visual noise
Open apple.com
Direction 2

Monzo

Best if you want friendly finance UI that still feels modern and clear.

  • Strong hierarchy and easy-to-scan sections
  • Rounded cards and approachable tone
  • Good inspiration for transaction history
Open monzo.com
Direction 3

Revolut

Best if you want a sharper, more high-end fintech feeling.

  • Darker accents with premium contrast
  • Confident typography and stronger density
  • Good reference for polished dashboard cards
Open revolut.com
Direction 4

Copilot Money

Best if you want SmartSpend to feel like a premium personal finance app.

  • Very strong app branding and visual polish
  • Excellent use of charts, spacing, and summaries
  • Good inspiration for onboarding and dashboard flow
Open copilot.money
Direction 5

Ramp

Best if you want a clean, data-heavy but still beautiful interface.

  • Great for layout structure and information grouping
  • Professional card design and spacing
  • Good reference for web dashboard patterns
Open ramp.com
Direction 6

Linear

Not a finance app, but excellent for sleek product design.

  • Super refined spacing and typography
  • Strong modern dark and light theme inspiration
  • Useful if you want SmartSpend to feel more premium-tech
Open linear.app
Direction 7

Wealthfront

Best if you want trust, simplicity, and a softer finance tone.

  • Warm but clean finance visual language
  • Good balance between data and calmness
  • Nice reference for landing and summary sections
Open wealthfront.com
Direction 8

Mint Alternatives Roundup

Useful if you want to compare how many consumer budget apps look in one place.

  • Good for spotting patterns you like or hate
  • Helpful for choosing between playful and premium
  • Lets us position SmartSpend more intentionally
Open roundup
